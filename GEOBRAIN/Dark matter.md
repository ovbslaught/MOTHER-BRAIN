- For a century, physicists tried to detect dark matter particles directly—and mostly failed[web:38]

**The Breakthrough:**
- Totani's team analyzed gamma-ray data from Fermi over more than a decade
- They looked for the signature of dark matter particles annihilating each other
- When a dark matter particle meets its antiparticle, they annihilate and release gamma rays[web:38]
- The spatial distribution of these gamma rays should form a distinctive halo pattern if dark matter is real[web:38]

**The Result:**
- They found a statistically significant halo of 20 GeV gamma rays
- The pattern **matches theoretical predictions for dark matter distribution**
